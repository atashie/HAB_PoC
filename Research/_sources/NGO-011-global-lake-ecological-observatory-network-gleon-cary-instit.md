## Methods
The page describes GLEON's core method as deploying high-frequency/high-resolution sensor instrumentation (e.g., monitoring buoys, illustrated by Lake Sunapee's buoy) on member lakes and then sharing/synthesizing that sensor data across a distributed, volunteer ("grassroots") global scientist network. One listed affiliated publication title (Hamilton et al. 2015) states the network's data are used "for validation of deterministic ecological models," indicating sensor data serve a model-validation role, though no methodological detail on how validation is performed is given on this page itself. The page also references a training pipeline (GLEON Fellowship/Lake Expedition Programs) that produces synthesis products from shared lake data, and points to one affiliated 2024 paper applying "knowledge-guided machine learning" to detect waterbody areal change, though again only the title is available here, not the method's internal workings, performance, or validation results.
