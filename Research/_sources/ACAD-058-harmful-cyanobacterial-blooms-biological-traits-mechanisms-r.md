# Harmful Cyanobacterial Blooms: Biological Traits, Mechanisms, Risks, and Control Strategies

> Note: provisional URL was resolved to a primary source. Original: https://www.annualreviews.org/content/journals/10.1146/annurev-environ-112320-081653?crawler=true

**What it is.** A 2023 peer-reviewed synthesis review (Annual Review of Environment and Resources, Vol. 48, pp. 123–147) by nine authors that analyzes the ecophysiological traits of bloom-forming cyanobacteria and their interactions with environmental processes to explain the drivers of harmful cyanobacterial bloom (CyanoHAB) initiation, outbreak, and persistence in lakes, estuaries, and reservoirs worldwide, and argues for science-based, environmentally friendly control strategies.

## Methods
This is a narrative literature-synthesis review, not a primary empirical study, dataset release, or statistical/ML model. Per the abstract, its method is to analyze published ecophysiological trait data for bloom-forming cyanobacteria and their interactions with environmental processes, and to use that trait-based synthesis to explain bloom initiation, outbreak, and persistence. No specific dataset, statistical test, or code/model is described in the retrieved text. Regarding where things 'work or fail': the abstract itself frames existing bloom-control techniques as not yet adequately evaluated or guided at large operational scales ("urgent need to evaluate and guide proper use of bloom control techniques at large scales"), i.e., a stated research-practice gap rather than a tested method with a reported skill score.
