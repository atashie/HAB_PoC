# Solutions for Algae Control (Algae Corner)

**What it is.** A SePRO Corporation marketing/product hub page ("Algae Corner") that sorts nuisance pond/lake algae into three identification categories (filamentous mat formers, planktonic forms, and bottom-dwelling macroalgae such as chara/nitella) and matches each to a tier of SePRO's own commercial algaecide/water-quality products (SeClear/SeClear G, Captain XTR, Komeen Crystal, PAK 27). It recommends routine early/preventive use of SeClear, escalating to stronger copper-based or non-copper products for established infestations.

## Key claims

(Product portfolio table / "Comprehensive Management Portfolio" > Captain XTR)

## Methods
This is not a study — it presents no scientific method, model, dataset, sampling design, or efficacy trial. It offers a qualitative decision framework only: (1) identify algae type by visual/ecological category (filamentous mat former vs. planktonic vs. macroalga); (2) match to a product — SeClear/SeClear G for early, routine/preventive programs (also claimed to remove phosphorus nutrients); Captain XTR (copper-based, chelated, with a surfactant package) for "quick knockdown" of established/tough infestations; Komeen Crystal (concentrated chelated copper) specifically for bottom-dwelling macroalgae (pithophora, chara, nitella); PAK 27 (non-copper, OMRI-listed, NSF-certified for drinking water) where copper must be avoided (sensitive fish such as trout/koi) or for selective blue-green algae control. No dose-response, efficacy percentage, trial, or comparative performance data are given to substantiate any product claim — the page states its claims (e.g., "only EPA approved combination," "go-to solution") without citing supporting studies.

## Tensions with other findings
This is vendor marketing copy asserting strong claims ("go-to solution for tough algae," "Only EPA approved combination algaecides and water quality enhancers," "unique surfactant package") with no cited efficacy trial, dose-response data, or independent verification — which is in tension with this dossier's evidentiary bar (traceable, quantitative, cited data) used for scientific HAB sources (e.g., EPA CyAN, Water Quality Portal). The page also uses "toxic"/"toxin" language loosely for cyanobacteria and other nuisance algae (e.g., Lyngbya, and a stated Cladophora-botulism link) without naming specific cyanotoxins (e.g., microcystin, anatoxin-a, saxitoxin, cylindrospermopsin) or citing regulatory guidance thresholds, which is less precise than public-health-oriented HAB literature (e.g., EPA cyanotoxin health advisories). Separately, the "Algae Corner" content hub bundles a non-algal organism (Bryozoans) alongside true algae topics, showing the page's practical scope is "nuisance aquatic organisms a pond owner might treat" rather than a strict algal taxonomy — a minor scope inconsistency worth flagging if this source is cited for algae classification. Given all of this, the page should be used only as vendor domain-context (product/vocabulary reference for a possible treatment-recommendation layer in Part B/C) and explicitly labeled as vendor-sourced, not as an independent evidentiary or quantitative source for Part A's model/claims.
