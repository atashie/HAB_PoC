# SePRO Corporation — Aquatics Products / SeClear (SeClear® Algaecide and Water Quality Enhancer)

**What it is.** A SePRO Corporation manufacturer/marketing web page for SeClear, a copper-based aquatic algaecide sold together with a phosphorus-locking "water quality enhancer" function in one product, intended for pond/lake nuisance-algae control.

## Key claims
*(each tagged with its blind-review verdict)*

- **[✓ verified]** SeClear is marketed as combining an algaecide and a water-quality enhancer in one product, positioned to replace routine stand-alone algaecide treatments.
  - *evidence:* Manufacturer's own positioning/tagline on the product page; a vendor marketing claim with no independent comparison or citation given on the page. (Product page, primary description/tagline)
  - *quote:* "the first aquatic algaecide and water quality enhancer in one designed to replace routine algaecide treatments"
- **[✓ verified]** SeClear's stated mechanism is that copper ions inhibit photosynthesis in algal cells (giving algae control) while simultaneously removing phosphorus from the water column.
  - *evidence:* Stated directly in the 'how it works' description; no dose-response data, chemistry detail, or citation is given on the page for either half of the mechanism. (Product page, mechanism/how-it-works section)
  - *quote:* "SeClear's copper ions inhibit photosynthesis in algal cells, providing fast-acting control, while simultaneously removing phosphorus from the water column."
- **[✓ verified]** The page states visible algae control occurs 3 to 7 days after application, with retreatment recommended once regrowth appears.
  - *evidence:* Direct timeline statement; no trial data, site conditions (temperature, dose, algae taxa, water chemistry), or variance are given to qualify this window. (Product page, application/performance section)
  - *quote:* "Control will occur in 3 to 7 days following application and water bodies should be retreated when regrowth appears."
- **[✓ verified]** SeClear is claimed to reduce in-water phosphorus levels with each application (a repeated/cumulative nutrient-reduction claim tied to dosing).
  - *evidence:* Stated as a benefit of the product's dual action; no phosphorus-reduction magnitude (mg/L or %) or duration of effect is quantified on the page. (Product page, benefits/description section)
  - *quote:* "reducing in-water phosphorous levels with each application"
- **[✓ verified]** Treated water is claimed to be safe for turfgrass/ornamental plants and usable for irrigation immediately after treatment.
  - *evidence:* Direct safety/use claim; no irrigation withdrawal interval, toxicology data, or supporting field study is cited on this page. (Product page, safety/use-restrictions section)
  - *quote:* "Water treated with SeClear is completely safe on turfgrass and ornamental plants and can be used for irrigation immediately."
- **[✓ verified]** SeClear is stated to be certified to NSF/ANSI/CAN 60, the standard for drinking-water treatment additives.
  - *evidence:* Certification credential asserted on the page; certificate number, scope, or testing lab are not reproduced on the page. (Product page, certification/regulatory section)
  - *quote:* "certified to NSF/ANSI/CAN 60 for drinking water"
- **[✓ verified]** The page claims control of a 'broad-range of algae species' and 'nuisance algae' without naming specific target genera or species.
  - *evidence:* Breadth is asserted but not itemized anywhere in the fetched content; no specific cyanobacteria or filamentous-algae genus/species is named on the page. (Product page, treatment-claims section)
  - *quote:* "broad-range of algae species"
- **[✓ verified]** SeClear Algaecide is a federally registered pesticide (EPA Reg. No. 67690-55) manufactured by SePRO Corporation, with copper sulfate pentahydrate as its labeled active ingredient.
  - *evidence:* Confirmed via a supplementary direct WebFetch of the EPA-hosted product label PDF, which legibly returned the registration number, manufacturer, and active-ingredient name; corroborated independently by a WebSearch aggregation citing the same registration number and active ingredient. This is regulatory/label information, not stated on the marketing page itself. ## Methods
This is a manufacturer product/marketing web page, not a scientific study: it presents no experimental methods, sample sizes, field-trial protocols, or statistical analysis, and cites no peer-reviewed research. Supplementary EPA/label documents were fetched separately in an attempt to obtain quantitative regulatory data (registration number, active ingredient, concentration); these are regulatory filings, not efficacy studies, and were only partially legible via WebFetch (both the SePRO-hosted 'SeClear-RTU_Label.pdf' and the EPA-hosted label PDF returned largely compressed/unreadable text, per the fetch tool's own output), so most label-level detail (signal word, full directions for use, precautionary statements, exact application rates) could not be verified verbatim in this session.

## Stated limitations
The page itself states no explicit limitations, caveats, or conditions under which its claims might not hold (typical of marketing copy) — e.g., nothing on water hardness/alkalinity/pH dependence (a known sensitivity for copper-based algaecides in general), temperature dependence, application dose, treated water-body size, or which specific algae/cyanobacteria genera were actually tested. No independent efficacy data, field-trial results, comparative-product benchmarks, or uncertainty range is cited for any performance claim (e.g., the "3 to 7 days" control window carries no stated variability or test conditions). Full precautionary statements, exact use restrictions, and directions for use exist only in the separately linked Label/SDS PDFs, which in this session returned largely illegible/compressed text on direct WebFetch, so those details could not be independently verified verbatim here.

## Tensions with other findings
As SePRO Corporation's own commercial product page for a proprietary copper-based algaecide, this source presents a curative, in-lake-treatment framing ("fast-acting control" within days) rather than the preventive, watershed/nutrient-load-reduction framing common in independent HAB-management literature. The page does not address — and this dossier can therefore only note the omission, not confirm or deny — topics actively discussed elsewhere in HAB literature, such as whether copper-induced lysis of cyanobacterial cells can release intracellular cyanotoxins, or copper's accumulation/toxicity to non-target aquatic life over repeated applications. Because SePRO is simultaneously the source and the seller of the product described, its efficacy, safety, and "first of its kind" claims should be treated as vendor assertions rather than independently validated findings, and should be corroborated against independent, peer-reviewed, or regulatory-agency sources before being used in any comparative or causal statement in the broader HAB analysis.
