# EPA Drinking Water Health Advisory Documents for Cyanobacterial Toxins

> Note: provisional URL was resolved to a primary source. Original: https://www.epa.gov/ground-water-and-drinking-water/drinking-water-health-advisory-documents-cyanobacterial-toxins

**What it is.** An EPA Office of Water webpage/document set that hosts the agency's non-enforceable federal Health Advisories (HAs) for two cyanobacterial toxins — microcystins and cylindrospermopsin — issued in 2015 to give states and drinking-water utilities numeric, age-stratified concentration guidance below which adverse health effects are not anticipated over a 10-day exposure; it also links three "Health Effects Support Documents" (literature reviews) covering anatoxin-a, cylindrospermopsin, and microcystins.

## Methods
This is a regulatory risk-assessment / guidance product, not an empirical study. Per the fetched text, EPA's Office of Water commissioned Health Effects Support Documents (HESDs) - literature-review compilations of chemical/physical properties, toxin synthesis, environmental fate, occurrence/exposure data, and health effects - for three cyanotoxins (anatoxin-a, cylindrospermopsin, microcystins). For two of those (microcystins, cylindrospermopsin) EPA then derived a numeric 10-day drinking-water Health Advisory concentration for two life-stage groups (bottle-fed infants/pre-school children ≤6 yr; school-age children through adults). I could NOT independently verify the specific quantitative derivation (reference dose, uncertainty factors, point-of-departure toxicology study, treatment-technique removal-efficacy data) from the primary technical PDF reports in this session: repeated WebFetch attempts on the full "microcystins-report-2015.pdf" and "cylindrospermopsin-report-2015.pdf" documents either returned an explicit "cannot extract legible text / corrupted binary PDF" result, or, on one occasion, returned internally-plausible but unverifiable figures (RfD 0.04 mg/kg-day; uncertainty factor 1000 as 10x10x10; NOAEL 40 µg/kg-day from a 10-day mouse study; >95% removal via chlorination/ozone) that could not be reproduced on a repeat fetch of the identical URL and that conflicted with a different set of figures found via WebSearch of a secondary (non-EPA) legal-review citation (LOAEL ~50 µg/kg-day in rats, Heinze 1999, composite uncertainty factor ~1000 built from 10 x 10 x 3.16 x 3.16). Given this inconsistency, none of those derivation numbers are reported as verified facts in this dossier entry. What IS confirmed via multiple clean, mutually consistent fetches (EPA fact sheet PDF, an archived EPA news release, and EPA's current HABs summary HTML page) is the final output values themselves (0.3/1.6 µg/L microcystins; 0.7/3.0 µg/L cylindrospermopsin), the 10-day exposure basis, and the non-regulatory/advisory status.

## Stated limitations
The source itself states the HAs are non-enforceable technical guidance rather than regulatory standards ("not regulations and should not be construed as legally enforceable federal standards"), and the values are explicitly scoped to a 10-day exposure duration, meaning the fetched text gives no basis for extending these numbers to longer or chronic/repeated bloom-exposure scenarios. EPA also states the HAs "may change as new information becomes available," signaling the 2015 values are treated as provisional rather than final; per the document index, however, all six linked documents remain dated 2015 with no revision evident in the material fetched. Only two of the three reviewed toxins (microcystins, cylindrospermopsin) received a quantitative advisory; anatoxin-a has only a qualitative Health Effects Support Document with no numeric value anywhere in this source. The federal HA does not preempt states, which may adopt different standards, so the national figures are guidance rather than a single enforceable nationwide limit. Separately, as a limitation of this fetch (not a limitation stated by the source): the full technical Health Advisory reports underlying the two numeric values - which would contain the reference-dose derivation, uncertainty factors, point-of-departure toxicology study, and any drinking-water treatment-technique removal data - could not be reliably extracted as legible text despite repeated attempts, so those deeper quantitative details are omitted here rather than risk reporting unverified or inconsistent figures.

## Tensions with other findings
This source sets ingestion-based, drinking-water-specific thresholds under an explicit 10-day exposure assumption; it is a distinct regulatory line from EPA's separate recreational-water HAB criteria (a differently titled EPA document surfaced in search results but not fetched or verified here), so these drinking-water numbers should not be read across to recreational/dermal-contact bloom-risk questions. Because the values assume only 10 days of exposure, they sit in tension with real-world bloom seasons that can persist for weeks to months, and the fetched text does not address cumulative or repeated-exposure risk over such periods. The explicit "may change" caveat, combined with the values' unchanged 2015 vintage in the document list, creates tension for any downstream tool, model, or dashboard that would treat 0.3/1.6 µg/L (microcystins) or 0.7/3.0 µg/L (cylindrospermopsin) as a permanently fixed, universally enforceable ceiling - the source itself frames them as revisable guidance rather than settled law, and individual states may set different numbers. This is a correlational/regulatory-derivation document, not a causal or predictive model; it says nothing about which environmental drivers cause blooms or toxin concentrations to rise, so it should not be conflated with driver/forecasting sources in this review.
