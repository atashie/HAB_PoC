# Application of Optical Remote Sensing in Harmful Algal Blooms in Lakes: A Review

## Methods
This is a literature-synthesis review, not a primary empirical/field study. Per its abstract, it synthesizes recent advances in optical remote sensing (ORS) technologies for HAB monitoring in lakes across three areas: (1) novel sensor development, (2) advanced data-analytics frameworks, and (3) integration of multi-scale satellite-airborne-ground observation platforms, and it critically evaluates spectral-signature identification methodologies. Per CrossRef/OpenAlex metadata it draws on roughly 224-230 cited references. The abstract itself does not specify a formal systematic-review protocol (search strings, databases searched, literature date-range coverage, inclusion/exclusion criteria) or name specific sensors, retrieval algorithms, or case-study lakes with quantitative results — that level of detail would appear in the full text. ## Stated limitations
Stated in the article's own abstract as "persistent challenges" facing optical remote sensing of HABs in lakes: (1) suboptimal spatiotemporal resolution, (2) atmospheric correction uncertainties, and (3) limited model generalizability across heterogeneous aquatic systems. The review frames machine learning, spatial-temporal data fusion, and high-performance sensors as still-emerging ("explored as potential solutions"), i.e., not yet established fixes for these limitations. Because only the abstract could be verified (full text was blocked — see fetch_notes), any further caveats, uncertainty bounds, or scope restrictions the authors state in the Discussion/Conclusion sections are unknown to this dossier and are not asserted here.

## Tensions with other findings
Full text was not retrievable, so this dossier cannot verify specific points of agreement or disagreement with other HAB sources. The one substantive tension the abstract itself surfaces is general but relevant: its stated limitation that HAB retrieval approaches show "limited...generalizability across heterogeneous aquatic systems" is a direct caution against treating any single band-ratio, chlorophyll-a, or cyanobacteria-index algorithm (calibrated on one lake or region, e.g., via EPA CyAN or similar) as transferable to other water bodies without local recalibration and validation — relevant context for weighing any other HAB-PoC source that reports a broadly "universal" retrieval algorithm or a single accuracy statistic without cross-lake validation. No other specific contradictions can be confirmed without the full text.
