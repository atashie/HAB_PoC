**What it is.** The Oregon Health Authority's live public portal listing current recreational-use health advisories for cyanobacteria (blue-green algae) harmful blooms in Oregon waterbodies, updated through the monitoring season (2026 season began May 4, 2026). It communicates advisory status and confirmed cyanotoxin measurements to the public.

## Key claims
*(direct fetch; verdicts reflect the single fetched page, not a blind second pass)*

- **[✓ verified]** Oregon operates a three-tier recreational advisory system: **Advisory (red)** = cyanotoxin levels confirmed by testing (or conditions indicating potential) to be unsafe for swimming/boating and unsafe for pets; **Precaution (yellow)** = bloom confirmed or cyanotoxins present but at levels safe for human recreation, though possibly still unsafe for pets; **Lifted (blue)** = testing shows cyanotoxins absent or the bloom visibly gone.
  - *evidence:* Advisory-level definitions stated on the page.
- **[✓ verified]** Advisories are grounded in confirmed cyanotoxin testing, with specific measured microcystin concentrations reported per waterbody (e.g., Agency Lake 16.9 µg/L; Detroit Reservoir 0.73 µg/L microcystin at time of access).
  - *evidence:* Per-waterbody toxin concentrations listed on the page. Note: the page reports measured values but does not itself restate the numeric safe/unsafe recreational thresholds.
- **[✓ verified]** Coverage is explicitly partial: only a fraction of Oregon waterbodies are monitored or sampled due to limited resources, and the absence of an advisory does not mean a bloom is absent.
  - *evidence:* Stated limitation on the page.
  - *quote:* "A waterbody with no recreational health advisory does not mean that a bloom is not present."
- **[✓ verified]** The program advises a precautionary default for unmonitored or visibly bloom-affected waters.
  - *evidence:* Public-guidance messaging on the page.
  - *quote:* "when in doubt, stay out"

## Data / numbers
- 2026 monitoring season start: May 4, 2026
- Example confirmed microcystin measurements at access: Agency Lake 16.9 µg/L; Detroit Reservoir 0.73 µg/L
- Advisory tiers: 3 (Advisory / Precaution / Lifted)

## Methods
Operational public-health surveillance/communication, not a research study: OHA issues, updates, and lifts recreational advisories based on confirmed cyanotoxin sampling and bloom observation, and publishes current status per waterbody. Where it "works": conveys confirmed-toxin risk and clear recreational guidance. Where it is limited: only a subset of waterbodies is sampled, so the portal is not a complete picture of statewide bloom presence.

## Stated limitations
The page explicitly warns that only a fraction of Oregon waterbodies are monitored due to limited resources, so no-advisory status must not be read as bloom-free; it directs the public to a precautionary "when in doubt, stay out" default. This is a candid coverage-gap statement directly relevant to framing where satellite/remote sensing could complement sparse in-situ advisory monitoring.

## Tensions with other findings
Consistent with other state programs (e.g., [[SLG-005]] Ohio, [[SLG-006]] California) in using tiered, toxin-confirmed recreational advisories, and with the broader monitoring-coverage gap documented across the literature: sparse, resource-limited in-situ advisory networks leave most waterbodies unmonitored — the coverage problem that satellite cyanobacteria detection (e.g., EPA CyAN) is positioned to help address, subject to its own small-waterbody resolution limits.
