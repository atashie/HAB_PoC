## Methods
Review/synthesis paper (no primary field data collection evident from accessible text). It surveys remote-sensing-based HAB identification, which the authors say rests on the spectral properties of water (indicators cited: chlorophyll-a (Chl-a) concentration and surface algal bloom area), and three families of prediction models: (1) data-driven/machine-learning models (e.g., artificial neural networks, random forest, long short-term memory) that learn relationships between environmental variables and bloom events from historical data; (2) process-based (mechanistic) models that simulate photosynthesis, nutrient uptake, and cell division to give mechanistic insight supporting targeted management; and (3) hybrid models combining the two, which the authors state "further improve the prediction of the timing and magnitude of algal blooms." The accessible text does not describe the review's own literature-search methodology (search terms, databases, date range, or number of studies screened), so that could not be verified.
