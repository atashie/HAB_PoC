# The Role of Climate Change in the Proliferation of Freshwater Harmful Algal Blooms in Inland Water Bodies of the United States

**What it is.** A peer-reviewed literature review (AMS article type "REVIEW") in Earth Interactions (Vol. 28, Issue 1) by D. Yvette Wiley (USGS South Central Climate Adaptation Science Center) and Renee A. McPherson (University of Oklahoma) that synthesizes prior published research on how climate-change stressors (warming, shifting precipitation) interact with nutrient loading to influence the frequency/severity of freshwater harmful algae and cyanobacteria blooms in U.S. inland waters, and surveys state/tribal monitoring-and-reporting policy approaches.
