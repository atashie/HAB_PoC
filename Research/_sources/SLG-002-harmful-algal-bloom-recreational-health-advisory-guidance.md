# Harmful Algal Bloom Recreational Health Advisory Guidance

**What it is.** A Utah state guidance page (DWQ, with DHHS, last updated 2023, "based largely on guidance from the...EPA") that defines two recreational-exposure advisory tiers — Warning and Danger — for cyanobacteria/cyanotoxin-affected waterbodies. It gives local health departments (LHDs, who hold legal authority to post advisories/close water) numeric cyanobacteria cell-density and cyanotoxin (microcystin, cylindrospermopsin, anatoxin-a, saxitoxin) trigger values, the recreational-contact guidance tied to each tier, the decision/reporting workflow, and the sampling protocol for lifting an advisory.

## Methods
Advisory status is determined operationally, not by remote sensing: (1) visual/site-investigation confirmation of a bloom by DEQ, LHD staff, or trained partners; (2) collection of water samples per a documented Standard Operating Procedure (Collection of Phytoplankton Samples during Harmful Algal Blooms, doc. DWQ-2023-005581) for laboratory measurement of cyanobacteria cell density (cells/mL) and concentrations of four cyanotoxins (microcystin, cylindrospermopsin, anatoxin-a, saxitoxin, all in µg/L); (3) SPATT (Solid Phase Adsorption Toxin Testing) passive-sampler bags per a protocols.io SOP, used as a supplementary toxin-detection method; (4) fish/wildlife carcass sampling (per a joint U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service / DWQ protocol) when a mortality event may be linked to cyanotoxins; and, since 2025, (5) a distinct percent-areal-coverage visual/photographic method (per DWQ-2025-003103) for benthic, mat-forming cyanobacteria, explicitly because the page states the water-column cell-density/toxin-concentration approach above "cannot be applied" to mats. No remote-sensing or satellite method is described anywhere in the fetched content; this is a purely in-situ sampling and lab-analysis workflow feeding a rules-based (threshold-exceedance) decision, not a statistical or machine-learning model.

## Stated limitations
The source itself states one explicit, hard limitation: the standard (planktonic) HAB thresholds and workflow described above "cannot be applied to evaluating risk at waterbodies where harmful algae mats are present," which is why a wholly separate 2025 guidance and metric (percent mat coverage) was created for benthic cyanobacteria. Beyond that, the fetched content does not itself discuss measurement uncertainty, sampling error, false-positive/negative rates for visual confirmation, or how conflicting indicator readings (e.g., a cell count in the Warning range co-occurring with a toxin reading in the Danger range) are formally resolved beyond the stated "any single indicator exceeded" rule. Separately, as the analyst compiling this dossier, I flag a limitation of my own extraction process rather than the source: repeated automated fetches of this page returned internally inconsistent tier attributions for a subset of numbers -- specifically whether the cyanobacteria cell-density Warning threshold is 20,000 or 100,000 cells/mL, whether the "4 µg/L" microcystin/cylindrospermopsin values belong to the Warning tier or to a separate lower/flowchart screening level, and which tier (Warning vs. Danger) the anatoxin-a value of 15 µg/L versus 90 µg/L belongs to. I resolved the microcystin/cylindrospermopsin/anatoxin-a/saxitoxin Warning-vs-Danger table values with higher confidence by cross-checking against EPA's independently compiled "State & Tribal Toxin Thresholds and HAB Advisory Decision Methods" page, which matched one of the three DEQ-page extractions exactly; however, the cyanobacteria cell-density tier split and the precise relationship between the page's "table" and its "flowchart" remain only partially resolved. A linked source PDF (DWQ-2023-008224, "Recommended HAB Advisory Thresholds") that likely contains the authoritative table was found via search but returned only a cookie-consent error page when fetched, so it could not be used to fully resolve this. Anyone requiring exact regulatory figures for the cyanobacteria cell-density thresholds should verify directly against that primary PDF.

## Tensions with other findings
This guidance's own design (requiring exceedance of ANY ONE of five largely independent indicators -- cell density or one of four distinct cyanotoxins -- to trigger an advisory, rather than inferring toxin risk from cell density/biomass alone) is consistent with, rather than contradictory to, the broader HAB literature finding that cyanobacterial cell density and toxin concentration are only loosely coupled (toxin quota per cell varies by strain, species, and conditions). This has a direct implication -- stated here as an inference, not as something the source itself asserts -- for satellite/remote-sensing-based HAB risk tools (e.g., biomass- or chlorophyll-proxy signals such as EPA CyAN): a remote-sensing signal calibrated only to cell density/biomass would not necessarily predict which waterbodies cross this guidance's toxin-based Danger thresholds (2,000 µg/L microcystin, 90 µg/L anatoxin-a, 75 µg/L saxitoxin) and vice versa, since Utah's own regulatory framework treats these as separate, non-substitutable lines of evidence. This is a reasoned inference for downstream design purposes, not a causal claim, and not a discrepancy the source itself discusses.
