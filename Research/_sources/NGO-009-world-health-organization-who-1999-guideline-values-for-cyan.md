# World Health Organization (WHO) 1999 Guideline Values for Cyanobacteria in Freshwater

**What it is.** A U.S. EPA summary webpage (part of EPA's Harmful Algal Blooms / HABs program) that presents WHO-developed guidance values for the relative probability of adverse human health effects from recreational (contact/ingestion/inhalation) exposure to cyanobacteria in freshwater, expressed as a three-tier alert framework keyed to cyanobacterial cell density and/or chlorophyll-a concentration, with the highest tier defined qualitatively by visible scum formation.

## Key claims
*(each tagged with its blind-review verdict)*

- **[✓ verified]** EPA's page summarizes WHO-developed guidance values expressing the relative probability of acute health effects from recreational exposure to cyanobacteria in freshwater, organized as three tiered 'Guidance Levels.'
  - *evidence:* Stated near-identically in both fetch passes as the overall framing of the page/table. (Page introduction / table header)
  - *quote:* "guidance values for the relative probability of acute health effects"
- **[✓ verified]** Guidance Level 1 ('relatively low probability of adverse health effects') is triggered at 20,000 cyanobacterial cells/mL or, alternatively, 10 µg chlorophyll-a/litre; it is derived from a human bathing epidemiological study and is linked to short-term outcomes such as skin irritation and gastrointestinal illness.
  - *evidence:* Both fetch passes independently returned this same value pair, derivation basis, and health-outcome linkage for the lowest tier. (Guidance Level 1 row of the main table)
  - *quote:* "20 000 cyanobacterial cells/ml" ... "10 ug chlorophyll-a/litre"
- **[✓ verified]** Guidance Level 2 ('moderate probability of adverse health effects') is triggered at 100,000 cyanobacterial cells/mL or 50 µg chlorophyll-a/litre; it is derived from WHO's provisional drinking-water guideline value for microcystin-LR together with data on other cyanotoxins, and carries potential for long-term illness with some cyanobacterial species.
  - *evidence:* Both passes agree on the values and on the derivation basis (drinking-water microcystin-LR value + other cyanotoxin data). (Guidance Level 2 row of the main table)
  - *quote:* "100 000 cyanobacterial cells/ml" ... "50 ug chlorophyll-a/litre"
- **[✓ verified]** Guidance Level 3 ('high probability of adverse health effects') is defined qualitatively by cyanobacterial scum formation in whole-body-contact areas, not by a specific cell-count or chlorophyll-a number, and its basis is inference from oral animal lethal-poisoning data plus documented human poisoning case histories, with acute poisoning risk noted.

  - *quote:* "Cyanobacterial scum formation (whole-body contact areas)"

## Data / numbers
- 20,000 (WHO/EPA render as "20 000") cyanobacterial cells/mL — Guidance Level 1 ("relatively low probability of adverse health effects") threshold
- 10 µg chlorophyll-a/litre — Guidance Level 1 alternative threshold (triggers same tier as the cell-count value; source uses "OR" between the two metrics)
- 100,000 (rendered "100 000") cyanobacterial cells/mL — Guidance Level 2 ("moderate probability of adverse health effects") threshold
- 50 µg chlorophyll-a/litre — Guidance Level 2 alternative threshold
- Guidance Level 3 ("high probability of adverse health effects") — NO numeric cell-count or chlorophyll-a threshold given; defined only qualitatively as cyanobacterial scum formation in whole-body-contact areas
- 1999 = year of the underlying WHO guideline values per the page's own title; 2003 = publication year of the WHO document ('Guidelines for safe recreational water environments. Volume 1, Coastal and fresh waters') that the EPA page cites as the source, described on-page as 'based on' the 1999 guidelines
- No baseline, sample size, confidence interval, or error bar is given anywhere on the page for any of these threshold numbers

## Methods
This page is not a primary study; it is a guideline-value summary/reference table, so there is no original statistical model, sample, or analysis method described. Per the page, each tier's threshold is instead grounded in a different type of prior evidence: Level 1's cell-count/chlorophyll-a threshold comes "from human bathing epidemiological study" (the specific study is not named or cited by author/year on this page); Level 2's threshold is back-derived "from provisional drinking water guideline value for microcystin-LR and data concerning other cyanotoxins" (i.e., a toxin-concentration guideline is translated into an equivalent cell-count/chlorophyll-a surrogate); Level 3 rests on "inference from oral animal lethal poisoning" data together with "actual human illness case histories." No sample sizes, effect sizes, confidence intervals, or statistical significance values are reported anywhere on the page for any of these derivations.

## Stated limitations
The page itself, as fetched in both passes, does not contain an explicit "limitations" or "uncertainty" section. No statistical uncertainty (confidence intervals, error bars, sample sizes) is given for any threshold. The underlying "human bathing epidemiological study" referenced for Level 1 is not named, cited by author/year, or linked on this page, so its scope and population cannot be assessed from this source alone. Level 3 has no numeric threshold at all, only a qualitative descriptor (scum formation), which the page does not caveat further (e.g., no guidance on how to consistently define/observe "scum formation" is given). The page does not state whether these values apply outside freshwater recreational contexts (e.g., drinking water use is only referenced indirectly, as an input to deriving the Level 2 threshold, not as a separate covered use).

## Tensions with other findings
(1) Title/citation mismatch: the source's own title frames these as "1999 Guideline Values," but the document EPA actually cites on the page is the WHO 2003 publication "Guidelines for safe recreational water environments. Volume 1, Coastal and fresh waters," described as merely "based on" the 1999 guidelines — a reviewer should not treat this EPA page as a direct restatement of a document literally titled/dated 1999 without checking WHO's original texts. (2) Metric substitutability: the source itself treats cyanobacterial cell count and chlorophyll-a concentration as interchangeable ("OR") triggers for the same tier at both Level 1 and Level 2, even though chlorophyll-a is a general phytoplankton-biomass proxy and is not specific to cyanobacteria — meaning the same guidance tier can be reached via a non-cyanobacteria-specific measurement, a structural feature of the framework itself (not an inference added here) that could create cross-study inconsistency versus frameworks keyed only to cyanotoxin concentration or to cyanobacteria-specific cell counts. (3) Unit-of-measure mismatch with satellite-based HAB monitoring (e.g., EPA CyAN, which retrieves cyanobacteria abundance/biomass indices from ocean-color satellite data rather than in-situ cell counts in cells/mL): translating a satellite-derived index into this framework's cells/mL or chlorophyll-a µg/L thresholds requires an additional calibration/validation step that this page does not address, since the page is scoped only to how in-situ recreational monitoring values map to health-risk tiers, not to remote-sensing retrievals.
