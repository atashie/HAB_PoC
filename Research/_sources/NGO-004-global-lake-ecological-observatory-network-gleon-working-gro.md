## Methods
This is an organizational directory page, not a methods or research paper, so it does not itself apply or validate a single method. It describes, at a high level, the methods/data modalities used by GLEON's constituent working groups: (1) high-frequency in-situ buoy sensor data, analyzed for repeatable pre/post-bloom patterns (Cyanobacteria WG); (2) remote-sensing analysis of broad-scale, climate-driven lake change (Remote Sensing WG); (3) ecosystem process modelling (Lake Modelling WG, established 2010 specifically to develop/apply such models across the GLEON network); (4) lake-metabolism calculations of gross primary production (GPP) and ecosystem respiration (R) plus carbon-cycle work (Lake Metabolism & Carbon Cycling WG); and (5) citizen-science data collection via partnership with NETLAKE (Community Science WG). The page reports no accuracy, validation, or performance statistics for any of these methods, and does not state where any of them 'work' or 'fail' — the Cyanobacteria WG's own framing treats the core buoy-pattern question as unresolved ('examines whether... exist'), not a demonstrated result.

The one notable interpretive tension is epistemic rather than factual: the Cyanobacteria working group frames pre/post-bloom patterns in physical buoy variables as an open, actively-investigated hypothesis ('examines whether... exist... in repeatable ways for the four major cyanobacterial groups'), not a settled or generalizable signal.
