# Cyanobacteria Assessment Network (CyAN)

**What it is.** CyAN (Cyanobacteria Assessment Network) is a multi-agency (EPA, NASA, NOAA, USGS, USACE) U.S. federal project that uses satellite ocean-color remote sensing (chiefly Sentinel-3 OLCI, plus historical Envisat MERIS, and since 2024 Sentinel-2 MSI and a Landsat surface-water-temperature product) to detect and quantify cyanobacterial/algal blooms in U.S. lakes and estuaries, and, since July 2024, to beta-test an experimental weekly cyanoHAB forecasting model, with results disseminated through a public web application and other interfaces.

## Methods
The page describes an operational satellite ocean-color remote-sensing pipeline rather than a single statistical/ML model: primary current sensor is Sentinel-3 OLCI (Ocean Land Colour Instrument), daily/weekly, 2016-present; historical continental U.S. coverage came from the Envisat MERIS archive (2002-2012 imagery, applied FY2017); a Landsat-derived surface water temperature product is distributed via USGS EROS; and since 2024 the program has added the higher-resolution Sentinel-2 Multi-Spectral Imager. Outputs are disseminated through the CyAN web application, EnviroAtlas, Report on the Environment, and NASA's Ocean Color Web, intended to feed 'expedient public health advisory postings.' Separately, since July 2024, EPA has been beta-testing an explicitly 'experimental' cyanoHAB forecasting model producing weekly forecasts for 2,000+ U.S. lakes. The page asserts the detection approach 'works' in the sense of being a 'standard and uniform approach for early identification of cyanobacterial blooms ... useful and accessible to stakeholders,' but this overview page provides no quantitative skill/accuracy figures for either the detection algorithm or the new forecasting model; a linked 2020 journal article title indicates algorithm evaluation was done against field-measured microcystin data, but its results are not reproduced here.

## Stated limitations
(1) Geographic scope is explicitly self-limited to 'the contiguous U.S.' in the program's own goal statement (page does not claim coverage of Alaska, Hawaii, or territories). (2) The new cyanoHAB forecasting model is explicitly labeled 'experimental' and described as only in 'beta-testing' as of July 2024, i.e., the source itself does not present it as validated or operational. (3) The 'original' Sentinel-3-based project is stated to have 'concluded in 2023,' with the page noting that while data will keep being published and reprocessed annually, that portion of the active project work is ending -- a caveat about the program's own continuity. (4) No quantitative accuracy, precision/recall, or uncertainty figures for bloom detection or forecasting are given on this overview page; algorithm evaluation against field microcystin data is referenced only via a 2020 journal-article title, without its findings being reproduced here.
