## Unsupported synthesis claims

  - problem: The claim of 'nearly every' is overstated. The tradeoff is well-documented for copper- and H₂O₂-based algaecides but is not equally applicable to all treatment categories. Alum treatment for internal loading does not involve cell lysis/toxin release. PAK 27 (non-copper) toxin-release profile is explicitly flagged in [PVT-004] as not independently evaluated. Biomanipulation ([ACAD-016]) does not involve chemical cell lysis. The tradeoff is concentrated in chemical suppression approaches, not across all subtopics.
  - suggested fix: Revise to: 'A recurring theme across chemical and some physical in-lake treatment sub-topics is a suppression-vs-toxin-release trade-off: killing or lysing cyanobacterial cells is not the same as neutralizing the toxin they contain, and several sources show these two outcomes can diverge sharply.' This correctly scopes the tradeoff to where it is actually documented.
